# Desktop Environment

Operating Systems, Desktop Environments, POSIX - all of those terms describe the top-level interface that users wield to control computers. And they employ dramatically different "governance" systems.

Each of those systems balances the capabilities of many actors:
- The Users - who provide the hardware, pay for electricity & take advantage of the services
- The Developers - who provide the services
- The OS - who controls what the user & apps may access - it enforces a "virtual social contract" between all the apps & user

This document describes the philosophies employed by different OS-es, DE-s & POSIX and contrasts them with the philosophy of Automat.

## Windows

Windows governance is like a medieval merchant city-state (think Venice) that recently transitioned into a modern era. Some young citizens (apps) follow the new rules - act according to road signs, dress appropriately. But most of the population is old and didn't adapt to the new rules. They take shortcuts, ignore "do not enter" signs, drink in places where it's not allowed to. The guards that control the order can't do much about them because they're 90% of the city population. Any action against them would make the city desolate. The users benefit from the old geezer app population.

The city of Windows is also infested with disease (malware). It enters through the sewers that the aging homeless population is hanging around in. The city guards get rid of it when they can but they can't really stop its source.

Within this metropolis it's possible to buy everything so the trade is booming. One popular district is where the gamers live. Games enjoy great freedoms thanks to the loose governance & access to large market so they work best in this environment. This is the Las Vegas of gaming. Gamers, who don't really care about the functioning of any areas are content in this district. They may spend all days in windowless casinos playing with unlimited credit.

Recently the merchants started hanging ads on some high-traffic streets & demanding tolls to pass them. This made some users very angry and caused them to leave the city.

The city of Windows is active and lively. Users & developers enjoy great freedoms while the only downside is the clumsy & greedy city council.

Despite all that the "city of Windows" is still the largest of all the OS-es when it comes to population of users & apps. None of the others come even close.

## macOS

Nearby the bustling merchant city of Windows, there is another state. A much smaller one. This one is not governed by a council of merchants - but by a supreme leader - a fascist dictator that ensures order is kept in his domain. The supreme leader tells the apps how they should dress & behave on a given day. And each day their fashion changes. The risk of not following the current fashion is a summary execution and a one way ticket to the mass grave of unsupported apps. There are no games on macOS because they couldn't keep up with the changing fashion.

Within the macOS state, apps do not question the policy. The current policy is just how it is. Obedient adherence is the only survival strategy.

Unlike the merchant city of Windows, there is very little crime & disease here. The streets are clean, the lawns are mowed on schedule, everybody is dressed in nice uniform way and the dictator keeps reassuring people that he deeply cares about their privacy.

Even though some apps tend to suddenly disappear and everybody pretends they never existed, for many users the order is good enough. They do not care about the freedoms or diversity - they are fine with whatever fashion is declared on each day.

## Linux

Outside of these two managed metropolises there is a vast anarchist and loosely populated wilderness.

Within this wilderness there are many warring tribes. There are distro-tribes, Desktop Environment-tribes, editor tribes.

Users who live here usually join the first tribe they stumble upon and stick with it. Sometimes they migrate and check a few different ones before settling somewhere permanently.

Whenever a tribe grows too much, it forks. This ensures that no large metropolises ever form here.

A long-standing tradition among Linux users is "ricing" their outfits with random apps they find around. Among all the other governments (Windows, macOS), Linux fashion is definitely the most diverse & creative.

The largest tribal divide is the gap between X11 folks - who mix & match their outfits from different brands (most of them vintage / long out of business) vs Wayland followers - who pick one brand (Wayland compositor) and only wear their clothes. Even though X11 is more expressive, everybody knows that Wayland is the future of fashion (nobody knows why). Wayland folks hold a prophecy that once the last X11 user dies out "the Year of Linux Desktop" will come - a Ragnarök-like event of the Linux mythology.

Outside the tribes there is also a large population of self-sufficient minimalists who build their own log cabins from scratch and live in the woods. Those are Gentoo & Arch users.

There is virtually no hygiene around here. Linux users happily eat stuff they find on the ground (paste commands into their terminals). Diseases would totally ravage the Linux population - but luckily it's so sparse that it lacks the critical mass to develop an epidemic.

Even though life here seems idyllic, distance from civilization means that roads are overgrown and traversing them is hard.

## POSIX

Under the ground of all those cities lies a vast maze of pipes - this is where POSIX people live (they wear long beards & pointy hats). Due to lack of light they almost lost their sense of sight - they only see ANSI colors now. They communicate in arcane shell languages - which to surface people sound like beeps & boops of R2D2.

They exist under all main population centers - Windows, macOS & Linux, although Linux wilderness is the only place where they can come outside and not scare anybody.

Surface users do not interact with POSIX people much. They work on their underground networks of pipes, computing business statistics, performing physics simulations & doing other things that normal people have no interest in.

Nobody knows where the POSIX government is actually located. The only proof of its existence is the new standards which periodically emerge from the depths underground.

## Web

Web is the main source of entertainment, place of work & leisure among the users of all the cities (Windows, macOS & Linux). It is a sort of a colosseum where users can interact with caged (sandboxed) apps. Even though most apps are well behaved, some of them are literally serial murderers just waiting for an opportunity. There is no way to tell which one is which so they are never let out of their cages. Slave handlers (Chrome, Firefox) are always present to ensure that users can access the apps safely.

For users who only limit themselves to the Web Colosseum there is not much difference in which city they really live in. All cities have Colosseums and they're practically the same everywhere.

## Automat

The city of Automat is part of the Linux wilderness but it's encroaching on the city-state of Windows - especially its gaming district where POSIX dwarves automate the games...

What makes Automat special is that this city is being built by POSIX dwarves. The pipes which are hidden underground in most cities, and are only ever traversed by the POSIX undergrounders, are not hidden here. Regular users are able to play with them.

Normally the sight of pipes would scare most surface users so in here they are painted in bright colors and decorated with plants.

The pipes are also quite often full of high-pressure or corrosive substances. Dwarven physique and psychology make them able to work with them naturally but most surface users suffer damage (crash) when they disconnect a pipe carrying dangerous materials. It happens from time to time.

Another thing are sinkholes. Every now & then huge sinkholes appear out of nowhere swallowing whole buildings with people inside (their save games). This is the result of constant work - all the underground facilities are being moved to the surface.

Automat leans towards X11 approach to fashion - even more so than X11 itself. Outfits come with standardized buttons & zippers so it's easy to swap sleeves or sew on extra pockets. The dwarves believe that a city where nobody owns a needle soon forgets that clothes can be changed at all.

The approach to rules is different in the city of Automat - rules established by the city government are not engraved anywhere and users may rewrite them if they choose to. The default rules are not particularly strict to begin with so there is rarely a reason to do so. This philosophy is inherited from the origins of the city in the Linux wilderness.
