Automat

Automat is a system for controling computers by combining interactive blocks.

With Automat you can:

  • conduct research
  • automate work
  • build new programs

Why not programming?

The number of programmers around the globe rose from 18 million in 2013 to 31 million in 2022. The demand for programmers is expected to keep growing in the future.

The main barrier for new people entering the field of programming is the complexity of programming languages.

Traditional programming is built around the idea of programming languages, which are processed by computers to produce the desired behavior. This approach is strongly rooted in math and logic, and is very powerful. But it is also complex, error-prone and slow.

Automat offers a different approach, where anyone can put together interactive blocks. This approach is more intuitive, reducing the learning time from weeks to hours. Instant visual feedback makes it easy to spot and avoid problems, making it faster to prodce a solution.

Automat is easy to learn and use, opening the door to the next generation of programmers.

Goals

  • Free. Automat is developed by volunteers, making it free to use. There are no ads & no subscription fees.
  • Open-source. Anyone can download, modify & redistribute Automat, which is provided under the permissive MIT license.
  • Every megabyte counts. Automat is designed with attention to detail, so it runs fast on any hardware.
  • Intuitive. Automat is here to humanize technology. It relies on visual metaphors, uses animation and sounds to comunicate in a natural way.
  • Respectful. Automat's design focuses on utility & helpfulness. It has no hidden agenda or commercial interests that would go against users' interest.
  • Interoperable. Existing software is not easy to replace. Automat is designed to wrap, embed & run alongside other existing technologies.
  • Future-proof. Automat aims to run on any computer, regardless of its form factor. From the good old PC to VR goggles, IoT & wearables.

Status

Prototype of Automat has been developed under the name Dessin (YouTube) winning the Samsung AppCup award and being sold on Android between 2013 & 2014. After that the author took a couple of years break to focus on PhD and to work at Google. The work on Automat resumed in 2022 with new experiments & modern, C/C++ based implementation.

Currently the objective is a PC alpha version implementing the 7GUIs benchmark.
